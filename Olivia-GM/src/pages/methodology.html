Tomorrow, Today.

Welcome to the future of connected vehicles.

 
1
 

Objective

To ideate and develop new connectivity services for GM drivers that are differentiating, own-able and feasible in the short and medium-term

 
4
 

Weeks of Research

The team kicked off the project by deep-diving the connected vehicle space, running desk research, primary expert interviews, and deep diving GM proprietary research in the connected vehicle space.

 
12
 

Project Deep Dived

The team analysed 4 years worth of Sylvain/GM mobility projects - from SUV driver studies to truck owners to global driver attitude studies, and mined these projects for relevant connectivity concepts.

 
771
 

Concepts Harvested

from the 12 projects analysed by the team, a total long-list of connectivity concepts was drawn up - each concept pivoted off a defined user need, and contained external data interaction.

 
24
 

Service Proposition

Using bespoke prioritisation and synthesis frameworks, the team synthesised the 771 concepts into a long-list of 24 potentials connected service propositions

 
5
 

Final Services

Using GM owned quantitative research filters combined with design thinking filters of viability, feasibility and desirability, the team honed down the long-lost to five final service propositions.
