By The numbers.

Methodology

 
1
 

Objective

Ideate and develop new connectivity services for GM drivers that are differentiating, own-able, and feasible in the short and medium-term.

 
4
 

Weeks of Research

The team kicked off the project by deep-diving into the connected vehicle space, running desk research, conducting primary expert interviews, and auditing GM proprietary research in the connected vehicle space.

 
12
 

Project Deep Dived

The team analyzed 4 years worth of Sylvain Labs/GM mobility projects and mined these projects for relevant connectivity concepts.

 
771
 

Concepts Harvested

From the 12 projects analyzed by the team, we drew up a total of 771 connectivity concepts. Each concept pivoted off a defined user need and contained external data interaction.

 
24
 

Service Proposition

Using bespoke prioritization and synthesis frameworks, the team synthesized the 771 concepts into a long-list of 24 potential connected service propositions.

 
5
 

Final Services

Using GM-owned quantitative research filters combined with design thinking, filters of viability, feasibility, and desirability, the team honed the long-lost down to five final service propositions.
